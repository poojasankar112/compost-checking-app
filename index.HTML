Information About Composting:

Composting is the process of breaking down organic waste such as food scraps, leaves, and grass into a nutrient-rich soil called compost. This happens naturally when microorganisms like bacteria and fungi decompose the materials over time. Composting is important because it reduces the amount of waste sent to landfills and helps recycle natural materials back into the environment. The compost created can be used to improve soil quality, helping plants grow stronger and healthier. By composting, people can protect the environment, reduce pollution, and support sustainable gardening and farming.
